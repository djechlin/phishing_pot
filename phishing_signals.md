# Phishing Email Signals

1. Urgency language ("immediate action required", "account suspended", "expires today")
2. Generic greeting ("Dear Customer", "Dear User") instead of recipient's name
3. Sender domain mismatch (display name vs actual email address)
4. Lookalike domains (typosquatting, homograph attacks)
5. Suspicious or shortened URLs
6. URL text doesn't match actual href destination
7. Request for credentials, passwords, or sensitive data
8. Threatening consequences for inaction
9. Grammar and spelling errors
10. Unexpected attachments (especially .zip, .exe, .html, .docm)
11. Reply-to address differs from sender address
12. Missing or invalid DKIM/SPF/DMARC authentication
13. Unusual sending time (outside business hours for "corporate" emails)
14. Impersonation of known brands or executives
15. Offers that seem too good to be true (prizes, refunds, free items)
16. Requests to bypass normal procedures or keep something secret
17. Pressure to act quickly without verification
18. Embedded forms requesting login credentials
19. Mismatched or low-quality logos and branding
20. Base64 encoded content hiding malicious payloads
21. Unusual X-headers or mail routing paths
22. Invoice/payment requests with unexpected bank details
